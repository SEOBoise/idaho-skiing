Idaho's grand ski resorts offer a winter wonderland for avid skiers and snowboarders alike. One of the iconic ski resorts in Idaho is Sun Valley, widely recognized as the first destination ski resort in the United States. Another gem in Idaho's ski resort collection is Schweitzer Mountain Resort, tucked away in the scenic Selkirk Mountains. Additionally, the resort provides excellent amenities and a vibrant après-ski scene, making it a popular choice among visitors.

FAQ

What is the biggest ski resort in Idaho?

The biggest ski resort in Idaho is Sun Valley Resort.

How big is Sun Valley Resort?

Sun Valley Resort covers over 2,000 acres of skiable terrain.

What amenities does Sun Valley Resort offer?

Sun Valley Resort offers a variety of amenities, including multiple ski lifts, terrain parks, cross-country skiing trails, dining options, lodging accommodations, and a spa.

What is the average annual snowfall at Sun Valley Resort?

Sun Valley Resort receives an average annual snowfall of around 220 inches.

Are there beginner-friendly slopes at Sun Valley Resort?

Yes, Sun Valley Resort has slopes suitable for skiers of all skill levels, including beginners.

Can snowboarders also enjoy Sun Valley Resort?

Yes, Sun Valley Resort welcomes snowboarders and offers terrain suitable for snowboarding.

Are there any other activities to enjoy at Sun Valley Resort besides skiing?

Yes, Sun Valley Resort offers a variety of winter activities such as ice skating, snowshoeing, snowmobiling, and sleigh rides.

How far is Sun Valley Resort from major cities in Idaho?

Sun Valley Resort is located approximately 150 miles northeast of Boise, the capital of Idaho.

Does Sun Valley Resort offer ski lessons?

Yes, Sun Valley Resort offers ski lessons for all ages and skill levels.

Are there any nearby accommodations for visitors to Sun Valley Resort?

Yes, Sun Valley Resort offers a range of accommodations, including hotels, lodges, and vacation rentals.
